Rather than rushing to build, this quarter focused on researching the right delivery format for the SEO Academy before committing to development. Three delivery approaches were evaluated and documented before picking a direction.

SEO Academy — 3 Delivery Approaches Evaluated

Approach 1 — Videos & Blog Posts

Not the primary solution
ProsCons
Visual — better than text for explaining complex UI steps Duplicates what's already in help articles — little added value
In-app accessible — users don't need to leave the tool Static and costly — breaks whenever the UI changes
No personalization — same content for every user regardless of their store's actual issues

Approach 2 — Intercom Fin with Contextual Buttons

Not the primary solution
ProsCons
Custom entry points — opening message triggered by a specific button Cannot read or analyze URLs — a dealbreaker for an SEO tool
Can generate examples if the user provides a product title Complex setup: tags → workflows → snippets → coded buttons in app
No way to give Fin different instructions per button

Approach 3 — Custom AI Agent (Internal Build)

Most promising — alignment needed
ProsCons
Can be programmed with specific SEO logic (length, keywords, accessibility rules) Higher initial development and integration effort
Can read and analyze a customer's page URL in real time — unlike Fin
True SEO expertise, not general-purpose AI responses

We need team alignment on direction before building. Andrii has an internal testing environment ready.

What the Data Tells Us

  • Open rates were strong in January (40–70%+) but collapsed in February when a spam filter issue cut OR to ~10–15%. March partially recovered to ~11–17%. Deliverability was the biggest variable — not message quality.
  • Best open rate: 71.9% (Jan Competitive, Step 2, VB) — still zero conversions. High OR doesn't predict action for this audience.
  • Gifts didn't drive conversion. In several cases, the no-gift variant outperformed: Feb Est (Test) VA (no gift, 16% OR, 2.1% CR) vs VB (with gift, 0% OR). Gift presence was more noise than signal.
  • CRs hovered between 0–2.4% across the quarter. The highest (4.7%, Jan New Stores VB) came from only 21 sends — too small to be meaningful. No segment cracked a reliable CR above 2%.
  • March Plug In AI segment was the largest at ~2,500 sends — the biggest bet of the quarter. OR 12–17%, CR 0.6%. Zero conversions. Scale didn't fix the channel's fit problem.
  • QSS vs. app link variants performed similarly — no meaningful conversion difference between sending to the app vs. the QSS landing page.
  • Conclusion: ~12,700 emails sent. Zero installs or purchases. The audience opens, occasionally clicks, but doesn't convert from cold outreach at this stage. Channel not the right fit — data fully documented if revisited.

Rock D

Design Onboarding to Maximize Retention

The goal is not to "show users around" — it's to engineer the moment they feel the product working. Three milestones:

  • Engineer the aha moment: Define the one action that makes a new user feel the app delivering value (e.g. seeing their first SEO score, fixing their first critical issue, generating their first AI title). Design the first session around reaching that moment as fast as possible.
  • Welcome call banner: Surface the welcome call offer at the right moment in the journey — not just on signup, but triggered when the user has enough context to know what to ask. Position it as the next step after the aha moment.
  • Native in-app onboarding tour: Guide users through the key setup steps inside the app itself — reducing friction, reducing support tickets, and reducing early churn.
  • Rethink Intercom outbound messages for new users
